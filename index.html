Culturally speaking....

I came to realize that what you experience and learn when going places is quite different based on the time spent there and why you were there in the first place. The tourist experience, even when you spend weeks at a given city, is quite different from the business experience. Work gives you a completely different perspective on things. Of course, a few days will not do the trick, but if you get to spend some decent time in a given place, take the bus to the office, work side by side over long hours as another one there, do some groceries shopping at the local supermarket, this kind of normal life starts to build in you a cultural intimacy that I was not ever able to get as a tourist.

And a completely different story is when you live in a place and cross the 3-years-adaptation barrier. No more cultural shock, no more the usual excitement for being at new place, you become something close to a local.
